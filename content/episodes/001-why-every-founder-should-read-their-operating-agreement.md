You signed the most important document in your company and you didn't read it.

I know, because I've seen it happen hundreds of times. A founder walks into my office with a dispute — a co-founder wants out, an investor is making demands, someone wants to change the equity split — and I ask to see the operating agreement. They pull it up on their phone. They've never read past page two.

This episode is about why that happens, what's actually in that document, and what you can do about it right now. No jargon. No billable hours. Just the truth about the document that controls your company.

## What we cover

- **The default rules problem** — What happens when your operating agreement is silent on something. Spoiler: your state's default LLC statute fills in the gaps, and you probably won't like its answers.
- **The four clauses that actually matter** — Capital contributions, profit distribution, management authority, and what happens when someone wants out. Everything else is window dressing.
- **Why lawyers write them this way** — The incentive structure behind operating agreement templates, and why "standard" doesn't mean "good."
- **The co-founder breakup scenario** — I walk through a real (anonymized) dispute where a two-person LLC dissolved because neither founder had read the buyout clause. It cost them both six figures.
- **The 30-minute audit** — A practical framework for reading your own operating agreement tonight, even if you have zero legal background.

## The question that started this

*If this document controls your company, your equity, your exit, and your liability — why did you sign it without reading it?*
