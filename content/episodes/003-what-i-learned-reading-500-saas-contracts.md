Over the last two years, I've read roughly 500 SaaS contracts. Software-as-a-service agreements, terms of service, master subscription agreements — the documents that govern every piece of software your business runs on.

Most of them are terrible. Not terrible in a boring way. Terrible in a "this clause could destroy your business" way. This episode is about the patterns I found, the clauses that should scare you, and the one thing almost every SaaS contract gets wrong.

## What we cover

- **The auto-renewal trap** — Why 89% of the contracts I reviewed had auto-renewal clauses with 30-day notice windows buried in paragraph 14. The math behind why vendors love this structure.
- **Data portability (or lack thereof)** — What actually happens to your data when you cancel a SaaS subscription. Most contracts give you 30 days. Some give you zero. A few claim ownership of your data entirely.
- **The limitation of liability shell game** — How vendors cap their liability at "fees paid in the last 12 months" while exposing you to unlimited risk. Why this is the single most important clause in any SaaS agreement.
- **Unilateral modification clauses** — The one sentence that lets a vendor change the entire contract by updating a webpage. It's in most of the tools you use right now.
- **The five red flags** — A checklist for reviewing any SaaS contract in under 10 minutes, based on the patterns I found across 500 agreements.

## The question that started this

*You run your entire business on software you don't own, governed by contracts you didn't read. What could go wrong?*
